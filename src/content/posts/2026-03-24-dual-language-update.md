## Newport News Neighbors: Cleaner, Safer, and Stronger

Hello, Newport News neighbors! 😊

Here's an exciting update from the City that directly affects the look and feel of our neighborhoods. Newport News has officially launched the **Good Neighbor Patrol Vehicle** — a smart, community-focused initiative designed to help keep our streets cleaner, our properties safer, and our neighborhoods more welcoming.

### What Is the Good Neighbor Patrol Vehicle?

It's a specially equipped city vehicle that drives through our neighborhoods and uses exterior-mounted cameras to document potential property maintenance issues that are visible from public streets and sidewalks. Think of it as a friendly, watchful neighbor making rounds for the good of the whole community.

Mayor Phillip Jones put it best:

> *"This is the next step in turning policy into progress. Residents told us that run-down and poorly maintained properties hurt their neighborhoods. City Council listened, acted, and now we're delivering solutions on the ground."*

### What Kind of Issues Does It Look For?

The vehicle is designed to spot common code violations such as:

- 🌿 Overgrown grass, weeds, or vegetation
- 🗑️ Trash, debris, or discarded items in yards or driveways
- 🚗 Abandoned or inoperable vehicles visible from the street
- 🏚️ Peeling paint, broken windows, or damaged exterior coverings
- 🚧 Fences, sheds, or other structures that are leaning or unsafe
- 🏠 Unsecured or deteriorating vacant buildings

### What Happens If a Violation Is Found?

Finding an issue does not mean immediate enforcement. A Codes Compliance inspector will first verify the issue in person. If confirmed, **the property owner will receive a written notice and has 30 days to correct the problem** before any enforcement action is taken. The process is fair, consistent, and respectful.

The program was launched in direct response to feedback from the 2024 Community Survey, in which **88% of residents rated tackling neglected and vacant properties as a top priority** — yet only 17% were satisfied with progress. The City heard you, and now it's taking action.

---

### 📋 3 Reasons This Matters to Our Community

**① It Raises Property Values and Improves Quality of Life**
A well-maintained neighborhood benefits everyone. When neglected properties are addressed consistently, the entire neighborhood becomes more attractive, safer, and more valuable — including your own home.

**② You No Longer Need to Confront Neighbors Yourself**
Uncomfortable about that overgrown vacant lot next door? You no longer have to approach the situation on your own. The patrol vehicle systematically monitors neighborhoods on a routine basis, so the city handles code enforcement — not you. Less awkward, less conflict.

**③ This Is Your Voice at Work**
This program exists because residents like you spoke up in the 2024 Community Survey. As the city says: *"Newport News Speaks. Leaders Listen. And Get Things Done."* This is proof that civic engagement creates real, tangible change.
